1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------
This report evaluates the predictions of all benchmarked machine learning and deep
learning models against AASHTO Standard Practice R 56 (Standard Practice for
Certification of Inertial Profiling Systems). Under this engineering standard,
a predicted roughness value is deemed compliant if its absolute error falls within
the envelope: max(0.25 m/km, 10% of True Ground Truth IRI).

3. KEY ENGINEERING FINDINGS
---------------------------
* Superior Standard Compliance: The Proposed 1D-CNN + PICA achieves an AASHTO R 56
  compliance rate of 18.39%, outperforming the leading tabular ensemble (CatBoost at
  15.34%) by +3.05 percentage points, and outperforming recurrent architectures
  (Bi-LSTM at 11.19%) by +7.2 percentage points.

* Robustness Across Error Tolerances: As demonstrated in Table VII (Tolerance Sensitivity),
  the spatial convolution architecture maintains its leadership across all operational thresholds.
  At a relaxed 20% tolerance, the Proposed 1D-CNN reaches 35.05% compliance compared to CatBoost's 30.74%.
  At a surveying-grade 40% tolerance, our model achieves 63.64% compliance, maintaining an almost
  10 percentage point lead over CatBoost (53.76%) and XGBoost (52.76%).

* Significance for Edge Deployment: The CDF Error Distribution plot visually confirms that the
  cumulative error curve of the Proposed 1D-CNN ascends much faster than boosting models,
  proving that spatial convolutional feature extraction provides superior reliability for low-cost
  smartphone-based pavement monitoring systems.
